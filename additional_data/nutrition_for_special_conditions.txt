**Nutrition for Special Health Conditions**

---

Supporting overall health through nutrition requires adjustments when managing specific medical conditions. This guide provides tailored dietary strategies for common chronic health issues like diabetes and high cholesterol.

---

### 1. **Diabetes (Type 2)**
**Goals:** Stabilize blood glucose, improve insulin sensitivity, prevent complications.

**Core Principles:**
- **Carbohydrate control:** Prioritize low-glycemic index (GI) carbs such as oats, legumes, whole grains, and non-starchy vegetables.
- **Meal balance:** Combine carbs with protein and healthy fats to reduce glucose spikes.
- **Consistent timing:** Regular meals and snacks help maintain stable glucose levels.
- **Fiber intake:** Aim for 25–38g daily to improve blood sugar regulation.
- **Limit added sugars:** Avoid sugary drinks and highly processed snacks.

**Suggested foods:**
- Leafy greens, berries, lentils, Greek yogurt, nuts, seeds, fish (especially fatty fish like salmon)

**Foods to limit:**
- Refined carbs (white bread, pastries), sweetened beverages, excessive saturated fats

---

### 2. **High Blood Cholesterol**
**Goals:** Lower LDL ("bad") cholesterol, increase HDL ("good") cholesterol, reduce triglycerides.

**Core Principles:**
- **Increase soluble fiber:** Helps reduce LDL levels. Found in oats, barley, apples, citrus fruits, and legumes.
- **Reduce saturated fats:** Cut back on red meats, butter, and full-fat dairy.
- **Eliminate trans fats:** Avoid hydrogenated oils, often found in packaged snacks.
- **Boost healthy fats:** Include sources of omega-3s like flaxseeds, walnuts, and fatty fish.
- **Plant sterols/stanols:** Natural compounds found in fortified foods that block cholesterol absorption.

**Suggested foods:**
- Oatmeal, avocados, olive oil, almonds, sardines, chia seeds

**Foods to limit:**
- Fried foods, processed meats, full-fat cheese, baked goods with trans fats

---

### 3. **Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)**
**Goals:** Reduce blood pressure, support heart health, decrease sodium intake.

**Core Principles:**
- **DASH diet:** Emphasizes fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and low-fat dairy.
- **Lower sodium:** Keep intake under 2,300mg/day—or 1,500mg for optimal results.
- **Increase potassium-rich foods:** Helps balance sodium and relax blood vessels.
- **Moderate alcohol:** Excessive drinking raises blood pressure.

**Suggested foods:**
- Bananas, sweet potatoes, spinach, low-fat yogurt, beans, beets

**Foods to limit:**
- Canned soups, salty snacks, processed meats, pickled foods

---

### 4. **Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) or Stomach Problems**
**Goals:** Reduce gastrointestinal discomfort, avoid triggers, support gut health.

**Core Principles:**
- **Low FODMAP approach:** Temporarily reduce fermentable carbs that cause bloating and gas.
- **Identify personal triggers:** Track symptoms to identify problem foods.
- **Smaller, frequent meals:** Prevent overwhelming the digestive system.
- **Probiotics:** Can improve gut flora and reduce IBS symptoms for some individuals.

**Suggested foods:**
- Zucchini, carrots, oats, rice, firm tofu, lactose-free dairy, bananas

**Foods to limit:**
- Garlic, onions, beans, dairy (for lactose-sensitive individuals), high-fructose fruits (like apples and pears)

---

### Final Thoughts
Always consult a healthcare provider or registered dietitian for personalized dietary recommendations, especially when managing a chronic condition. Nutrition plays a powerful role in improving quality of life and supporting long-term health.
